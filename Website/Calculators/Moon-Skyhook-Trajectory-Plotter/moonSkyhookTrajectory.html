Geocentric Earth-Moon view. The Moon is placed from a Meeus low-precision lunar ephemeris (position good to ~arcminute). The skyhook is a gravity-gradient (radial) tether rotating once per orbit, so a point at radius r moves at ωr. The released trajectory is integrated (RK4) under Earth + Moon + Sun gravity, with the Moon and Sun at their real ephemeris positions (Meeus), so the SOI transition is continuous. The Earth-SOI v∞ and the orbit's primary, apsides and inclination are read from that integrated path (inclination is against the ecliptic once Earth or the Sun is the primary, or against the skyhook's own orbital plane while still Moon-bound after a waypoint burn). The Sun's disk is a marker in its true direction at a capped distance (the real Sun is far off this view). Up to two waypoint burns (prograde/radial/normal, km/s) can be added along the trajectory; each burn is resolved in the velocity frame of whichever body locally dominates at that point (the Moon during a lunar flyby, otherwise Earth, or the Sun once well past Earth's SOI), so a burn placed near a close flyby correctly shows an Oberth-boosted effect. Plane change is always measured against the ecliptic.

- Holds markdown files for each technology design job

- In-process designs are in the 'In process' sub-folder

- Other .md files are basically complete, but could be changed based on knock-on effects of other infrastructure changes, or new research that impacts previous decisons

- The vast bulk of research and design docs are scattered across other folders on this computer, and are being moved in here as they can be split into digestible chunks, summarized, and updated.

- The absence here of some item that is mentioned elsewhere means either the design docs have not yet been moved over, the design in Kim's head has yet to be recorded, or the design is provisional and will be either adopted or discarded later.

## Worldbuilding assumptions

- There are virtually no people involved in industry or transport. Robotic equipment and a range of AIs do all the work and almost all the oversight.

- Space industry has developed exceedingly advanced abilities to leverage hard vacuum and low to micro gravity to produce materials that are impossible or expensive on Earth. They produce a wide variety in bulk. Chief of these are:
  
  - Silica fiber with tensile strength of 17 GPa and density (with aluminum sizing) of 2.3 g/cm3. Produced in nearly flawless strands hundreds of meters long. This enables the first skyhooks.
  
  - CNT fiber with tensile strength of 42 GPa and density (with protective coatings and atoms that facilitate cross-linking) of 1.4 g/cm3. Produced in nearly flawless strands hundreds of meters long. This enables later skyhooks and space elevators.
  
  - Graphene in sheets up to many meters square, as little as 5 layers deep. Further properties TBD

- The moon is the hub of space industry, with extensive facilities on the surface and in orbit on the skyhooks.
  
  - It exports mostly finished products, not raw materials or basic components
  - It imports certain advanced components from Earth, and chemicals that aren't available economically from other sources - argon, lead, tin, mercury, iodine, and such

- Investment in massive infrastructure that takes 25 years to pay for itself, but requires fewer consumables, is preferred to infrastructure that diverts more resources to consumables that provide no profit.
  
  - Fuel production vs. fixed launch infrastructure is the main case
  - The calculation of investment amounts is very different, as the vast majority of the resources used in construction and fabrication come from offworld - both labor and materials.

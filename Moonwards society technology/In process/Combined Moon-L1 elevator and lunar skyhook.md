# Combined Moon-L1 space elevator and gravity gradient lunar skyhook

The aim is to find the lowest cost, most robust, most efficient approach to high-throughput cargo shipping, between a set of solar system bodies that together supply everything needed for extensive industrial capacity in cis-lunar space. 

## Goal:

### Momentum trading

If there are skyhooks around the moon and Earth, plus the L1 elevator, it may be possible to set up an ongoing exchange of masses simply for the purpose of returning lost momentum, to the skyhooks, especially the lunar one. If this can be done, it would mean only a small set of engines would need to be on the skyhook top decks. They would only be there for backup and orbital fine-tuning. Ships that are basically boulders with small engines (for course correction or docking) would instead transfer momentum to the skyhooks. They would constantly shuttle around among orbits designed for momentum transfer. This would probably be a far simpler system to build and maintain - even if likely far larger and heavier, that could be a good trade.

### Minimal mass driver use

Though a mass driver may well still be useful enough to justify building one on the moon's equator, likely it could be smaller, shorter, and see less traffic. Instead most cargo climbs up the elevator, and transfers to the lunar skyhook, if desired, by dropping off the elevator at an altitude that puts it in a good orbit to rendezvous with the skyhook at the appropriate altitude.

### Greatly reduced use of energy and fuel for transport

If the previous two points can be achieved, the amount of energy and fuel that would need to be produced for the purpose of transport in cis-lunar space, and locally on the moon, would be much reduced. Instead, the amount of material suitable for very high tension loads would need to be much greater. Even so, over time the cost and burden of such material production would likely be far lower than the cost and burden of producing fuel and energy purely for transport, plus the maintenance of engines and all the related infrastrucuture.

## Investigation:

Are there easy ways to put momentum transfer ships into orbit, after release high on the elevator, such that they dock easily with the lunar skyhook, travel down its tether a good distance (passing momentum to the skyhook as they go through the Coriolis force), and can then be released into an orbit that can easily dock again with the elevator, high up close to L1?

Is it feasible to anchor the elevator to the moon's surface at a number of points far from the equator - perhaps 20° N and S, and then have those various cables converge so far above the equator, that the skyhook can pass between them and never be at risk of colliding with the elevator's cables?

How best can these two systems be combined to achieve the needed delivery of cargo to Earth, and traffic between the moon and Ceres, Psyche, and Mars?
